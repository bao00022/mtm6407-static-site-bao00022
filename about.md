# About

## Who I Am

I'm an Interactive Media Design student with a growing passion for photography. What started as a curiosity during my studies gradually became something deeper, a way of seeing the world differently.

I believe photography is less about technical mastery and more about intention. I pick up my camera when something catches my eye: a slant of light, a quiet street, a face lost in thought. For me, every shot is an attempt to uncover the beauty that already exists, and to preserve a moment that would otherwise slip by unnoticed.

## My Gear

My camera of choice is the Canon EOS 60D, a DSLR that's been around for a while, but still delivers exactly what I need. I'm not a gear person. I don't chase the latest bodies or collect lenses for the sake of it. The 60D is reliable, familiar, and honestly, its limitations have taught me more about photography than any upgrade ever could.

Good photos come from observation, not equipment.

## My Style

I'm drawn to simplicity. No heavy edits, no dramatic filters. Just the scene as it was, shaped by light and timing.

My approach is quiet and unhurried. I'd rather wait for the right moment than force one. Whether I'm shooting a vast landscape or a small detail in nature, I try to let the subject speak for itself. The camera is just there to listen.
